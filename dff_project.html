Design Fabric Festival

The inaugural edition of the Design Fabric Festival was held from March 29–31, 2018 at Famous Studios in Mumbai. We were asked to design the festival identity by the founder and our great friend, Sanket Avlani. The event was initiated with the goal to raise the standards of the burgeoning Indian design scene and put Mumbai on the map of global design hubs. It featured 30 national + international artists, and included talks, workshops, exhibitions and gatherings.


The Design Fabric Festival identity was inspired by the design elements of Mumbai Suburban Railway. In order to capture the spirit of Mumbai in our design, we looked at the chaos, cacophony, people and several other subjects that represent the city. However, nothing came close to the theme of Mumbai Locals. Carrying over 7.5 million daily commuters, they are the lifeline of the city. We were not only inspired by them but also wanted to pay a tribute via our design.

  • Shrenik Ganatra and I got the opportunity to present the process behind the development of the identity at Design Fabric Festival.

    • A dynamic identity made sure that the identity remains fresh throughout the promotions and the duration of the festival.

  • The stripes are at the core of the system. Playing with the width and direction of stripes—the variables, we were able to create a dynamic system that generates infinite variations of the identity. The system of stripes also doubles as a grid for the typographic and visual elements within the identity.

    • The dynamic identity and the typographic system gives room to play with the visual language and create variety of collaterals while maintaining the core aesthetics.

  • The booklet was a beautiful interpretation of how digital and analog can combine. It was provided during the festival to know the schedule which also acted as a booklet to jot things down.

  • The wesbite was an integral part of the festival for announcing new speakers and schedules. One of the main considerations while designing the website was providing an experience that is equivalent to the physical world which was achieved through micro-interactions of the identity system.
